Color Usage Rules
Do
  • Use primary (ember) sparingly â€” only for CTAs, active states, and key highlights. Restraint is what makes it powerful.
  • Keep 90%+ of your UI in the neutral ramp (background, card, muted, border). Let the neutrals do the heavy lifting.
  • Use accent (soft ember tint) for hover states, selected rows, and subtle emphasis â€” never as a primary surface.
  • Use semantic colors only for their intended state (success = success, not "go" or "positive").
  • In dark mode, use the brighter ember (#FF6A3D) â€” the light-mode muted ember lacks contrast on dark backgrounds.
Don't
  • Never use ember as a large surface fill (full-width banners, entire card backgrounds). It overwhelms the palette.
  • Never use pure black (#000000) or pure white (#FFFFFF) for backgrounds. Always use the designated off-tones.
  • Don't mix semantic colors with brand colors â€” no ember-tinted error states, no green primary buttons.
  • Avoid ember text on light backgrounds below 16px â€” it fails WCAG AA contrast at small sizes.
  • Don't introduce new accent hues. The palette is intentionally single-accent. New hues dilute the brand.
